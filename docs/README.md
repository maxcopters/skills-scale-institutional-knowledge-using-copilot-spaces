# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview
OctoAcme manages its cross-functional projects with a disciplined, iterative process focused on delivering customer value, with defined roles and transparent collaboration.

### Project Management Lifecycle
Projects move through five structured phases: Initiation (validating business needs and alignment), Planning (breaking work into shippable increments and setting priorities), Execution & Tracking (incremental delivery, regular standups, code reviews, and quality gates), Release & Deployment (pre-release requirements, smoke tests, rollback plans), and Retrospective (learning and improvement). Each phase is supported with clear workflow guides and templates for consistent, repeatable execution.

### Roles and Collaboration
Key roles include Project Managers (coordinating delivery, risks and comms), Product Managers (owning vision and backlog), Developers (building and testing), QA/Testing (validating feature quality), and Stakeholders (inputs and approvals). Accountability is distributed, with regular syncs between PM and Product Lead to maintain alignment.

### Communication and Quality
Communication is structured (daily standups, weekly and monthly updates, demos, and risk reviews). Risks are tracked in a dedicated register and escalated through defined paths. Quality is maintained through automated/unit/integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, and mandatory code review, with small PRs and strong QA practices.
