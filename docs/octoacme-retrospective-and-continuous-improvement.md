# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload

## Roles in Retrospectives

- **Project Manager**: Facilitates the retrospective, ensures action items are captured and tracked
- **Product Manager**: Provides context on product outcomes and customer feedback
- **Tech Lead**: Discusses technical improvements and architectural learnings
- **QA Lead**: Shares quality insights and test strategy improvements
- **Developers**: Share implementation insights and blockers
- **Security Owner**: Discusses security findings and prevention measures
- **Support/Ops Lead**: Shares operational insights and incident learnings (if applicable)

Action items are assigned to specific roles with clear owners and deadlines.

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync
